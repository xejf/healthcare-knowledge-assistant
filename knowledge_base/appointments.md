# Appointment Handling Procedures

*Fictional sample document for the Sunrise Valley Health Clinic training project.*

## Booking a New Appointment

1. Verify the patient's identity (see "Verifying Patient Identity" below).
2. Ask for the reason for the visit in general terms (e.g., "check-up", "follow-up"). Do not ask for detailed medical information at the front desk.
3. Check provider availability in the scheduling system.
4. Offer the patient at least two available time slots when possible.
5. Confirm the appointment date, time, provider name, and location back to the patient.
6. Record the booking in the scheduling system and send the automatic confirmation message if the patient has opted in.

New patients must also be asked to arrive 15 minutes early to complete registration forms.

## Rescheduling an Appointment

1. Verify the patient's identity before making any change.
2. Locate the existing appointment in the scheduling system.
3. Ask for the preferred new date or time and check availability.
4. Move the appointment and confirm the new details back to the patient.
5. Cancel the old slot so it becomes available to other patients.
6. Note the reschedule reason in the appointment record if the patient offers one (never pressure them to explain).

If a patient has rescheduled the same appointment more than three times, add a note to the record and inform your supervisor so the care team is aware.

## Canceling an Appointment

1. Verify the patient's identity.
2. Confirm which appointment the patient wants to cancel (date, time, provider).
3. Cancel the appointment in the scheduling system.
4. Ask whether the patient would like to rebook now or later.
5. Record the cancellation. Same-day cancellations must be flagged so the slot can be offered to waitlisted patients.

Staff must never cancel an appointment without the patient's (or authorized representative's) request, except when instructed by a provider or supervisor.

## Verifying Patient Identity

Before discussing or changing any appointment, confirm at least TWO of the following:

- Full name
- Date of birth
- Phone number on file
- Address on file

If the caller cannot verify their identity, politely explain that changes can only be made by the patient or an authorized representative, and offer to have the patient call back directly.

## When to Escalate Appointment Issues

Escalate to your supervisor when:

- A patient requests an urgent same-day appointment for a concerning health issue — also follow the emergency escalation protocol if symptoms sound serious.
- There is a double-booking or scheduling system error you cannot resolve.
- A patient becomes angry or distressed about scheduling.
- A caller asks you to change another person's appointment without authorization.
- A provider requests a schedule change that conflicts with clinic policy.
