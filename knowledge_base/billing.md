# Billing and Insurance Direction Guide

*Fictional sample document for the Sunrise Valley Health Clinic training project.*

## How Staff Should Handle Billing Questions

Front desk and support staff act as a **first point of contact and direction** for billing questions — not as billing decision-makers.

When a patient asks a billing question:

1. Listen fully and stay professional, even if the patient is frustrated.
2. Verify the patient's identity before discussing any account details.
3. Answer only with the general information staff are allowed to explain (see below).
4. For anything beyond that, connect the patient to the Billing Department (extension 2600, open Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM) or provide the billing office callback form.
5. Document the interaction and where the patient was directed.

## What Staff CAN Explain

- How to read the basic sections of a clinic statement (date of service, amount billed, amount due).
- Accepted payment methods: card, cash, or the online patient portal.
- That payment plans exist, and that the Billing Department sets them up.
- How to update insurance information on file.
- How to request an itemized statement from the Billing Department.
- General clinic policy: co-pays are collected at check-in.

## What Staff CANNOT Decide or Promise

Staff must never:

- Decide or predict whether insurance will approve or cover a service.
- Waive, reduce, or change any charge or fee.
- Set up or modify payment plans (Billing Department only).
- Interpret the details of a patient's insurance policy.
- Promise refunds or billing corrections.
- Discuss another person's billing information without authorization.

If a patient pushes for any of these, respond: "That decision is handled by our Billing Department — let me connect you with them so you get an accurate answer."

## When to Refer to the Billing Department

Refer the patient to the Billing Department when they:

- Dispute a charge or believe there is a billing error.
- Ask whether insurance will cover or approve a procedure.
- Want a payment plan, discount, or financial assistance information.
- Need an itemized statement or billing records.
- Have questions about a collection notice.

For patients who are upset about a bill and become distressed or angry, follow the de-escalation steps in the patient service guide and involve your supervisor.
