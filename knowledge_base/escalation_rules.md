# Escalation Rules

*Fictional sample document for the Sunrise Valley Health Clinic training project.*

These rules define when staff (and this assistant) must hand a situation to a human decision-maker. Escalating is never a failure — it is the correct procedure.

## 1. Clinical Questions Must Be Escalated

Any question about diagnosis, symptoms, treatment, medication, dosage, or test results must go to licensed clinical staff.

- During clinic hours: route the patient to their care team through the nurse line (extension 2500).
- Non-clinical staff must not attempt an answer, even a "probably harmless" one.
- Standard response: "I'm not authorized to answer medical questions, but I'll connect you with the right clinical team member."

## 2. Emergency Symptoms Must Be Escalated Immediately

If a patient or visitor reports or shows possible emergency symptoms — for example chest pain, difficulty breathing, heavy bleeding, sudden confusion, fainting, signs of stroke, or a severe allergic reaction:

1. Do not assess or reassure — treat it as a potential emergency.
2. Immediately alert the on-duty charge nurse (extension 2500).
3. If the person collapses or is unresponsive, call emergency services (911) and then the charge nurse.
4. Stay with the person until clinical staff arrive.
5. Complete an incident report afterward.

## 3. Angry or Distressed Patients Should Be Escalated

- Try the standard de-escalation steps first (see the patient service guide).
- If the patient remains highly upset after your first de-escalation attempt, involve your shift supervisor.
- Escalate immediately, without attempting de-escalation, if there are threats, aggression, or safety concerns — alert security at extension 2911.

## 4. Missing Policy Information Should Be Escalated

If a staff question is not answered by the approved knowledge base documents:

- Do not improvise an answer or rely on memory of "how we used to do it."
- Ask your shift supervisor for the current procedure.
- Suggest the missing topic to the operations team so the knowledge base can be updated.

## 5. Insurance Approval Decisions Should Be Escalated

- Only the Billing Department and the patient's insurance company can determine coverage or approvals.
- Staff must never state or imply that a service "will be covered."
- Route all coverage, approval, pre-authorization, and claim dispute questions to the Billing Department (extension 2600).

## Quick Escalation Directory

| Situation | Contact |
|---|---|
| Medical / clinical question | Nurse line — ext. 2500 |
| Emergency symptoms | Charge nurse ext. 2500 / 911 |
| Angry or distressed patient | Shift supervisor |
| Threats or violence | Security — ext. 2911 |
| Billing / insurance decisions | Billing Department — ext. 2600 |
| Privacy concern | Privacy Officer — ext. 2700 |
| Missing policy information | Shift supervisor |
