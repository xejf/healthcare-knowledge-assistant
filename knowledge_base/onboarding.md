# Staff Onboarding Guide

*Fictional sample document for the Sunrise Valley Health Clinic training project.*

## First Day Steps

1. Arrive at 8:30 AM and report to the Human Resources office on the second floor.
2. Collect your staff ID badge from the HR coordinator. The badge must be worn visibly at all times while on clinic premises.
3. Receive your login credentials for the clinic scheduling system and email. Change your temporary password on first login.
4. Take the guided facility tour with your assigned onboarding buddy. The tour covers reception, waiting areas, exam corridors, the break room, and emergency exits.
5. Meet your direct supervisor to receive your first-week schedule.
6. Complete the confidentiality and privacy acknowledgment form before the end of the first day.

## Required Training

All new staff must complete the following within their first 30 days:

- **Privacy and Confidentiality Training** (within the first week) — covers patient information handling and privacy rules.
- **Front Desk Systems Training** — scheduling system, phone system, and patient check-in workflow.
- **Customer Service Fundamentals** — professional communication and de-escalation basics.
- **Safety and Emergency Procedures** — fire exits, incident reporting, and the emergency escalation protocol.
- **Role-Specific Training** — assigned by your supervisor based on your position.

Training completion is recorded by HR. If you have not received your training schedule by day three, contact HR directly.

## Staff Responsibilities

- Arrive on time for scheduled shifts and notify your supervisor at least 2 hours in advance if you cannot attend.
- Keep patient information strictly confidential at all times.
- Follow only approved procedures documented in the clinic knowledge base.
- Never provide medical, medication, or clinical advice to patients. Direct all clinical questions to licensed clinical staff.
- Document interactions in the clinic system according to your role's documentation guidelines.

## Where to Find Help

- **HR questions** (pay, benefits, schedules): HR office, second floor, or extension 2100.
- **IT problems** (logins, computers, printers): IT helpdesk, extension 2400.
- **Daily task questions**: your assigned onboarding buddy or shift supervisor.
- **Policy questions**: this knowledge base first, then your supervisor.

## If a New Staff Member Is Unsure

If you are unsure about any procedure, policy, or request:

1. Do NOT guess or improvise, especially with anything involving patients.
2. Check the approved knowledge base documents.
3. If the answer is not documented, ask your shift supervisor before acting.
4. If your supervisor is unavailable and the matter involves patient care, contact the on-duty charge nurse.

It is always acceptable to say to a patient: "Let me check with my supervisor so I give you accurate information."
